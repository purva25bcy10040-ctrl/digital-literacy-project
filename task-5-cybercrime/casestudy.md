 Case Study: UPI Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students and working professionals in India. In this type of fraud, scammers trick victims into sharing sensitive details such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by sending fake payment requests.

A typical incident begins when a fraudster calls or messages the victim pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. They may claim that there is an issue with the victim’s bank account or offer a fake reward or refund. The scammer then sends a UPI “collect request” and instructs the victim to enter their UPI PIN to receive money. However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment, resulting in money being transferred from the victim’s account.

Students are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps for daily transactions. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and misuse of personal banking information. Many victims realize the fraud only after the money has already been deducted.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness and careful handling of online financial transactions.
